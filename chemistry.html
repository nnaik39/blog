Organic Chemistry and Watermelons
LEVEL 1: CHEMISTRY FOUNDATIONS
Nomenclature
Some basic organic chemistry terms.
Definitions A carbonyl functional group is a C-O double bond. A carboxyl group is a C-O double bond, with the C also bonded to an OH.
Properties A C-O double bond is polar. Polar molecules are very attracted to each other, so members of the group have a comparatively high melting point. Soluble in water.
Aldehydes and Ketones Aldehydes and ketones are both organic compounds in the carbonyl functional group. An aldehyde is on the end of a carbon chain, while a ketone is in the middle.

Aromaticity Rules
What makes a molecule smelly?
Aromaticity Rules
CYCLIC The last carbon connects to the first carbon to form a ring(see benzene.)
CONJUGATED Every atom in the ring must be able to have resonance. So an atom with four bonds is out.
HUCKEL'S RULE The hydrocarbon must have n pi electrons, where n is in the sequence 2,6,10,14...(4n+2).
FLAT Molecular geometry must be planar, so no tetrahedral carbons (sp3 orbitals), etc.

Aromaticity and MO Theory
The aromaticity rules can be restated in terms of p orbitals. Why do they work?
Aromaticity and MO Theory
Delocalization A delocalized pi bond is a pi bond where the electrons are free to move over more than two nuclei. Electron delocalization happens when p orbitals in a flat, cyclic structure overlap. Delocalized electrons are more stable because they spread energy over a wide area rather than keeping it bottled up in a small one.
Aromaticity For a molecule to be aromatic, there must be a shell of overlapping p orbitals.

LEVEL 2: APPLICATIONS TO WATERMELONS
Watermelon Constituents
What chemicals are inside a watermelon?
Main Watermelon Constituents
Lycopene gives watermelon its red color. It has a formula of C40H56. It is a carotenoid, which is a class of plant pigments in biology derived from carotene. (so an alkene) It is not aromatic.
Citrulline, C6H13N3O3, is an amino acid. Amino acids are carboxylic acids with an amine group attached. Amines are derivatives of ammonia. After you ingest watermelon, citrulline turns into arginine and nitric oxide (NO.)

Aromaticity in a Watermelon
Which compound contributes to the watermelon aroma?
Aromaticity in a Watermelon
COMPOUNDS The compound (Z,Z)-3,6-nonadienal is thought to be the main watermelon odourant, as well as some other aldehydes like (Z)-3-hexenal. It is not aromatic, but still rather smelly.
WHAT do those names mean? The empirical formula for (Z,Z)-3,6-nonadienal is C9H14O. More nomenclature details in Additional Info.
DOES it really smell, if it is not aromatic? (Compounds that are not aromatic are aliphatic.) Aliphatic aldehydes are used a lot in perfumes. They were made famous by the perfume Chanel No. 5.

Alcohols Inside a Watermelon
How is an aldehyde turned into an alcohol?
Alcohols Inside a Watermelon
WHAT Over time, enzymatic action converts (Z,Z)-3,6-nonadienal to an alcohol. This basically consists of tacking on two more hydrogens, and rearranging the molecule so it has an OH functional group.
PROPERTIES This has a nucleophilic addition reaction and produces the alcohol (Z,Z)-3,6-nonadien-1-ol. Initially, chemists thought that this alcohol was the main odourant of watermelon since their instruments were too slow to catch the aldehyde.
